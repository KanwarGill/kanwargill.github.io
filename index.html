About Me

I came to Toronto in the year 2010 to pursue my undergraduate education at the University of Toronto. Over the course of my education and through many internships I have gained incredible technical and interpersonal skills. I have mastered the principle of self-learning which is so vital when working as an intern in a company for as small a period as four months. I have worked in environments such as the federal government, to a financial organization, all the way to a multinational software giant. Working in these diverse companies, I have learned one thing – If you don't feel scared you're not going fast enough. Challenging yourself to change each day is what drives me to perform at excellence and outlast myself day after day. I am always open to new and exciting ideas. Feel free to reach out to me!

While focus on theoretical courses such as the theory of computation and computability & complexity is at the core of this stream, there are other courses too where you get to learn about software principles and hardware. Research Work

University of Toronto

Department of Computer Science

NSERC USRA Researcher • May - August 2015

Under the supervision of Professor Bianca Schroeder and PHD student Ioan Stefanovici worked on a project pertaining to characterizing the caching behavior in distributed cloud applications. Wrote a cache simulator in Python that worked under many different policies such as LRU, LFU, CLOCK, etc. when run against the OLTP traces for producing hits against the cache.

Leadership

Co-op Students' Association

President • May 2014 - April 2015

The Co-operative Students’ Association (CSA) is one of the leading student organizations at the University of Toronto at Scarborough. We are one of the most academically diverse student groups in the university, representing over 1600 students from the Arts & Sciences Co-op and Management Co-op. As the President of CSA, my main focus was to boost campus outreach among all the students and advocate the humongous benefits of the Co-op Program. Working closely with the Arts & Science and Management Co-op departments on campus, the events were planned specifically to benefit Co-op students on campus. I managed a team of 7 senior executives who further managed their associates. The overall team effort for the year was tremendous and we received outstanding recognition from the Co-op departments as well as the students on campus.

Executive Advisor • May 2015 - April 2016

UTSC Scarborough Toastmasters

Co-Founder and VP Membership • January 2014 - April 2015

I got to know about Toastmasters while at IBM when I joined their club. Upon my return to school and discovering that there wasn't a Toastmasters club in our campus I alongwith two other passionate individuals founded the club. The response has been tremendous and a lot of students have become a member of the club since. It's an honor for me to say that I have finished the first level in the program in both communication and leadership! I'm now a Competent Communicator (CC) and a Competent Leader (CL).
